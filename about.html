"It's more than skin deep."

Currerntly drummers are restricted to shaping drum sound through drum heads, dampening, & stick selection.  When we design your drums we can alter the other three elements of the envelope to "produce" your sound, this beffefits the overall experience of not only the player but the band & audience.  

SOUND IT OUT

Much like how we can breakdown a sentance into words & words into letters, we can also break down music  into sounds waves. The sound waves are then mesured in 4 main sections, commonly refered to as an envelope or A.D.S.R.

Attack 

When you first strike a drum the Attack refers to the time it takes for your sound to go from silent to the loudest level. 

Sustain 

Sustain refers to the level during the main sequence of your sound. This is the moment when the shell material & heads are most engaged, drummers often refer to this as tone, color, or timber.

Decay

The Decay time controls how long it takes for your sound to go from the peak of attack to the sustain level. 

Release 

Release controls how long it takes your sound to return to silence after the sustan cycle.
